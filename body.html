EARS

1. Resting: If the ears of a cat look relaxed, this means the cat is calm.

2. High & Up: If the ears of a cat stand up straight, something has caught the cat's interest and is now alert.

3. Down: When the ears of the cat are pushed back or flat, the cat is either angry or scared.

EYES

1. Large/Dilated: When the eyes of a cat dilate, something has caught the cat’s attention.

2. Narrow & Constricted: When the eyes of a cat look like thin ovals, the cat is angry.

3. Slow Blinks: When a cat is slowly blinking at you, the cat feels relaxed and trusts you.

BODY

1. Normal: When a cat is calm, the body of the cat is relaxed.

2. Arched: If a cat’s body looks like an arch and the fur stands straight up, the cat feels agitated and/or angry.

3. Crouched: When a cat curls into a ball or stays low to the ground, the cat is scared. Cats do this as a defense mechanism, protecting their vital organs from any possible threats.

4. Lied Back: When you come across a cat that exposes its belly to you, congrats! This means the cat is really comfortable around you. But be careful! You might want to pet the belly of the cat, but you’ll likely end up getting scratched!

TAIL

1. High: When a cat's tail is resting high, this usually means the cat is happy or friendly.

2. Down: When a cat’s tail is down and tucked between its legs, the cat feels anxious and/or scared.

3. Flicking: When a cat's tail starts to sway back and forth, the cat feels upset/angry.
